# Picks Reflections

Post-game reflection log. Every settled pick gets a short entry.
Goal: build real pattern recognition over time, not just a W/L scoreboard.

## Format

Each entry answers:
1. What was the stated edge thesis?
2. What actually decided the game?
3. Was the data available to catch it — did I look?
4. Bad bet or bad result?
5. What changes going forward?

## 2026-05-16 — Phillies ML over Pirates — W

1. Stated edge thesis: Philadelphia had aligned form, starter, and bullpen edges; -170 / 0.61 Polymarket was acceptable for that gap.
2. What decided it: Cristopher Sánchez erased bullpen risk entirely with a 9.0-inning shutout, 6 hits, 0 walks, and 13 K. Bubba Chandler failed the opposing-starter floor with 4 walks and 5 runs/4 ER in 3.0 IP, and Harper's 1st-inning three-run homer gave the Phillies immediate control.
3. Was it catchable: yes — this followed the exact pregame thesis, with starter dominance even stronger than required.
4. Bet/result quality: good read, good result.
5. Change: none. Keep requiring aligned starter/form/bullpen evidence before paying road favorite chalk.

## 2026-05-15 — Pirates ML over Phillies — L

1. Stated edge thesis: Pittsburgh had the cleaner starter path with Ashcraft over Nola; price was acceptable if the Pirates bullpen was survivable.
2. What decided it: the starter read was right early — Nola allowed 6 ER in 3.2 IP and Pittsburgh led 7-3 after five. The bullpen read failed: Soto/Santana allowed 6 runs from the 9th on, turning an 8-5 lead into an 11-9 extras loss.
3. Was it catchable: yes. The card named Pirates bullpen walk traffic as the hold-up, then still treated it as survivable without a leverage-map stress test.
4. Bet/result quality: bad read / missed signal, not pure variance.
5. Change: add a bullpen walk-traffic veto for favorites/tight-game locks; do not let a starter gap cover an unverified late-inning walk problem.
