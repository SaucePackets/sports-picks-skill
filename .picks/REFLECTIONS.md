# Picks Reflections

Post-game reflection log. Every settled pick gets a short entry.
Goal: build real pattern recognition over time, not just a W/L scoreboard.

## Format

Each entry answers:
1. What was the stated edge thesis?
2. What actually decided the game?
3. Was the data available to catch it — did I look?
4. Bad bet or bad result?
5. What changes going forward?

---

## 2026-04-21 — TOR @ LAA (L)

**Pick:** Angels -115
**Result:** TOR 4, LAA 2

**Edge thesis:** Angels had the better recent run-prevention trend, the number looked fair in a near coin-flip spot, and Toronto on the road with Corbin did not look trustworthy enough to justify backing the Jays.

**What decided it:** Toronto played the cleaner offensive game and Angels bats never created real damage. The game was 1-1 into the 8th, then Toronto broke it open with a 2-run double and an RBI single. Final team shape: **TOR 4 runs, 8 hits, 3 walks, 2 doubles, 4 strikeouts** versus **LAA 2 runs, 5 hits, 2 walks, 0 extra-base hits, 6 strikeouts**. This was not a fluky one-inning ambush. The Angels simply did not have enough offensive force in a fairly even game.

**Was the data available to catch it?** Partially. We had the recent run-prevention trend, but the offensive edge for LAA was never strong enough to support an official pick on its own. We also knew bullpen was not fully verified and still let the game through as official. That should have pushed the read closer to pass.

**Bad bet or bad result?** Bad bet, or at least too thin for official-card status. The handicap leaned too much on distrust of Toronto and not enough on a clear winner-first case for the Angels.

**What changes:** Do not make an official pick mainly on recent run prevention if the offense does not have a convincing damage path. If the case is mostly "their pitching trend is better" plus "I do not trust the other side," that is usually a pass. Also, when bullpen is unverified and the offensive edge is only average, downgrade hard or stay off the game.
