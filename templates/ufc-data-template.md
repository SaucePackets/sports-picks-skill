# UFC Betting — Data & Analysis Reference (Template)

Structure for building UFC-specific betting knowledge.

---

## Betting Markets

### Moneyline (ML)
Pick the fighter to win. Standard American odds.

### Over/Under Rounds
- **1.5:** Fight ends before halfway through round 2
- **2.5:** Fight ends before halfway through round 3 (standard for 3-round fights)
- **3.5 / 4.5:** Used for 5-round title fights

### Method of Victory
- KO/TKO — Referee stoppage due to strikes
- Submission — Fighter taps or goes unconscious
- Decision — Judges' scorecards
- DQ — Disqualification

### Prop Bets
- Fight to go the distance
- Fighter by specific method
- Exact round betting
- Points handicap

---

## Key Fighter Data Points

### Striking
- Significant strikes landed/attempted per minute
- Striking accuracy % (60%+ is elite)
- Striking defense % (60%+ is elite)
- Knockdowns per 15 minutes
- Striking differential (landed minus absorbed)

### Grappling
- Takedown average per 15 minutes
- Takedown accuracy %
- Takedown defense %
- Submission average per 15 minutes

### Experience & Trends
- Age (28-33 is prime; over 35 decline risk)
- Strength of schedule
- Durability (has chin been tested?)
- Recent form (HOW they won/lost, not just W/L)
- Weight cut history
- Layoff length (6-12 months normal; 18+ = ring rust)

---

## Betting Patterns & Rules

### Heavy Favorites (-300+)
- ML has no value at these prices
- Bet method of victory or Under rounds instead

### Near Pick'ems (-150 to +150)
- Most actionable range
- Look for stylistic mismatches the market missed

### Underdogs (+200+)
- Usually live early (round 1) or not at all
- "Live dog" criteria: opposite style, never been finished, massive physical advantage

### Round Betting
- Under 1.5: Two power punchers, two grapplers, or massive tier gap
- Over 1.5: Technical strikers, durable grapplers, slow starters
- Title fights (5 rounds): Over 3.5 if both fighters have cardio
